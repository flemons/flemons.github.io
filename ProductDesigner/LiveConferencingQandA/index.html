Live conferencing Q&A

Q4 events is an online conferencing product that digitizes the in-person experience of the annual general meeting. This is a yearly event that allow shareholders to hear results. upcoming projects, vote, and ask questions.

The annual general meeting has extensive red tape, specific actions and accessibilities that need to be given to every user, in every meeting. If these requirements are not met, companies can incur large financial penalties.

Role

Product Designer | UXR


Company

Q4 Inc.


Duration

4 weeks

Background

Annual general meetings are very stressful situations for public companies. This is a once a year meeting where their shareholders can ask (almost) any question, and vote on critical company decisions.

The Q&A in particular is a chaotic point with potentially hundreds of questions submitted that need to be sorted and selected in a matter of minutes. Any mistakes in missing/fielding questions have costed companies billions.

Process

Scoping

Reframing

Initially scoped as a feature request, working with the PM we re-aligned the project to the user problem: How might we enable our users to answer questions from their audience?. Aiming broad to start, the problem statement let the team explore all options in both research as well as ideation.

Planning

At this point in the project, the full product was in it’s infancy. Working with other designers, timelines were set using a workback. This broke the overall new product into it’s smaller components.

With leadership this also set expectations for overall results, indicating the short time frame for both definition and testing.

Research

Approach

With short timelines, end users were going to be difficult to wrangle. Because of this, reliance on recordings, secondary research, and internal domain experts was taken. In reviewing the recordings, external research, and 1 on 1 interviews I sought to learn:

  • Understanding Q&A in AGM’s, the process, the purpose, the participants
  • Understanding who are the key users, and their problems
  • Understanding legal requirements
  • Understanding technical domain

Learnings

  • Managing Q&A in multiple places: Understanding the lifecycle of a question raised concerns of the importance of collaboration and complexity of controls in products today.
  • Lack of flexibility: The rigidity in many of the steps in the AGM surfaced the difficulty in how many different users and viewpoints are necessary.
  • Difficulty in decision making: Because questions are commonly submitted live, decisions had to be made off the cuff on what would and would not be answered.

Requirements

Based on the learnings, working with the PM we identified the minimum functional requirements, as well as areas that had room for improvement. One focus for myself was also future-proofing this product, over-indexing into AGM could make it difficult to scale to other shareholder meeting contexts.

UI design

Task flow

Guiding ideation was a Q&A-specific task flow diagram. This baked into a larger task flow that illustrated all of the actions users could take across the project.

This was considered in the context of the primary user archetypes, including the two primary perspectives of a host versus an attendee.

Wireframing

Using the in-person experience as a starting point, wireframing explored concepts from competitors to completely independent products. The primary source of inspiration ended up being music players andqueueing tracks.

The biggest difficulty came in the changing of data depending on the user. Companies wanted to limit access and availability to some information, but the rigid requirements of AGM’s mandated certain info.

The designs of presenters and attendees changed drastically due top this requirement, resulting in a far more simplified attendee experience

Testing

Utilizing lower fidelity wireframes to get ad-hoc feedback, internal experts gave feedback on what worked and what didn’t. Their feedback focused on the necessity for simplicity, and for getting as much done as possible before the event started, constantly comparing it to a live event.Development review also revealed some minor changes to be made, both in scaling and interactions.

Refinement

All primary actions were given clear labeling and single placements. The list itself however was as flexible as possible to not hinder queueing in any way. This included allowing for click and drag actions, discrete received, queued, and answered columns, as well as easy fallbacks to revert actions.

The lifecycle of a question carried through most design decisions, with both the aforementioned columns of a question state, as well as pre-event controls to allow as much time as possible for users to review questions before answering the audience. From an attendee perspective, their interface was extremely stripped back with all actions for the user constrained to one place (Q&A, voting, additional materials). Users were also given transparency into their questions by viewing simplified states.

Results

In data

Testing revealed positive results, including Increased speed of finding questions by 30% and a 100% positive sentiment about the solution for Q&A.

With the completion of the Q&A, the product went on to secure multiple Fortune 500 companies to host AGM’s on the platform, as well as aiding in a multi-million dollar deal with the largest host of shareholder meetings in the world.

Learnings

  • Balancing customer to users needs: At multiple times in this project, the major organization we were building it for expressed preference for not giving data to attendees. Compromises were made to still allow users the required data, and additional data would be held elsewhere.
  • Necessity for mobile: With the primary focus on the host's desktop experience, any mobile experience was given lower priority, making it difficult to scale at certain points. A mobile-first approach would be applied to combat this in the future
