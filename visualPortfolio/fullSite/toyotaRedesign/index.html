Toyota Website Redesign

Toyota Canada website research and redesign

Brief

Toyota is a multi-billion dollar automotive manufacturer with branches located across the globe. With this comes an expectation of being a leader in all aspects of it's brand. Unfortunatly the Canadian facing website for Toyota is extremely out of date.

The purpose of the project was to re-design the Toyota Canada website to better match current web standards.

Problem

Toyota is currently one of the top manufacturers sold in Canada. Despite this, the multi-billion company has neglected to update their Canadian facing website past the mid-2000â€™s. While material on the site is current, the layout is outdated and difficult to navigate for new users.

The dated look also reflects poorly on the brand, especially in an industry with such a reliance on being as modern as possible.

Solution

The new redesign better fits with current web design trends, using more simplistic visuals and layout, as well as spreading out the content to make the most of the available space.

Just as previously mentioned, the update also changes the viewpoint to a more modern company that Toyota strives itself to be.

Current Website Analysis

Pain Points

  • No call to action, nothing immediatly grabs the attention of the user.
  • Very bland site, no memorable components.
  • Very small content window, with very little of the page being used.
  • Buttons blend in with the navigation bar and side bar. Models don't appear interactive.
  • Available sales disappears when models are interacted with.
  • Does not stand out from other car manufacturer websites.

Positives

  • Easy to move to relevant page for specific car models.
  • Content is front and center.
  • Content small, but clear & concise.
  • Layout of the content is consistent.

Wireframes Explorations

Navigation bar located in the center, much like the current page, with important information easily accessible further down the page. This was far too condensed in terms of information, and did not follow the design principle of ease of use. Any menu would require mutltiple clicks to get to a users preferred page.

Another layout tested was a 4 panel layout, having each main menu button extremely large for visual aesthetic and accessibility. However it again condensed the information too much, and left much of the page unused, much like the sites current state at the projects start.

For my third wireframeI looked at exploring a more simple layout, following current design trends. The advantage to using this type of design, primarily in a high traffic and wide demographic setting, is recognition. No tutorial or guiding lines are necessary, the eye is immediatly attracted to the nav bar, giving titles for each segment of the information.

The idea of having the rest of the information contained at the bottom was a constant thought, as instinctively, users familiar with traditional website layouts move to the bottom. However the content section needed some more eye grabbing information, so this design was further refined.

Keeping with the concept of standing out among the competitors, the final wireframe concept uses a car choosing quiz. The quiz helps narrow down choices to a customers parameters, giving them a clear view of what vehicle would fit best into their life.

The layout also sticks with the design requirements of having all the models visually available right as the user lands on the page. The overview with simple information can be viewed immediately, with more information on specific models with a single click.

High Fidelity Mockup

The final website layout was chosen due to it's familiarity. With the majority of users who navigate to the Toyota Canada website at least competent in technology, the more the familiar the site, the easier it will be for users to navigate.

Also keeping with some of the concepts thought of in the previous stage, a "car chooser" was added. The thinking behind this is that new car buyers have issues deciding on what a vehicle is for them. People dislike going into dealerships and asking the questions on the chance they might be pushed to buying a car. This tool will negate any issues surrounding car buying for research. Coupled with 3 very simple questions for users to choose from, the tool makes it easy for users to research what car fits them best.

The tool also keeps the quick access abilities the original website gave. This layout is more efficient as it not only seperates the vehicle entities into their own entities, it add's a visual element of the car. This visual element also can be used for marketing purposes, having bright colour attributed to newer models or models that may be on sale.

The issue with information overload was also considered in the final mock-up. Cutting down the amount of main menus, and moving the information down to the bottom makes it easier to understand navigation. The overview of every single model available was also removed in order to not confuse any user, and left space for the revolving banner at the top.

As this was only a landing page rework, I decided from the information located on some pages could be condensed. Pages such as hybrids and Environment contained a minimal amount of information. User's searching for this information will still be able to find it under a different relevant category. Eliminating pages and sub menus makes a far less cluttered and difficult experience, especially as the majority of the pages are not viewed by the typical client.
